# Opportunity Zones

## Overview

Qualified Opportunity Zones (QOZs) were created by the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 to encourage long-term investment in economically distressed communities. Investors can defer and reduce capital gains taxes by investing those gains into Qualified Opportunity Funds (QOFs), with the potential for tax-free appreciation if held long enough.

## Key Benefits

- **Deferral**: Defer recognition of capital gains until 2026 (or earlier sale)
- **Reduction**: No step-up in basis remaining after 2026 (previously 10% at 5 years, 15% at 7 years)
- **Exclusion**: Exclude 100% of appreciation on QOF investment held 10+ years
- Geographic flexibility with 8,700+ designated zones

## Tax Benefit Summary

| Benefit | Requirement | Status (2026) |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Gain Deferral | Invest gain in QOF | **Ends 12/31/2026** - All deferred gains recognized |
| 10% Basis Step-up | 5-year hold | Expired (needed investment by 12/31/2019) |
| 15% Basis Step-up | 7-year hold | Expired (needed investment by 12/31/2017) |
| Exclusion of QOF Appreciation | 10-year hold | Available through 2047 (for 2026 investments held 10+ years) |

## Effective Dates

| Provision | Effective Date | Expiration |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| QOZ Designation | Tax Cuts and Jobs Act December 22, 2017 | Zone designations through 2028 (most zones) |
| Capital Gains Deferral | December 22, 2017 | **December 31, 2026** |
| 10% Basis Step-up | Required 5-year hold | Expired - Required investment by 12/31/2019 |
| 15% Basis Step-up | Required 7-year hold | Expired - Required investment by 12/31/2017 |
| 10-Year Appreciation Exclusion | 10-year hold from investment | Active - Available for investments held through 2036+ |
| Working Capital Safe Harbor | Final Regulations 2020 | Active |

**2026 Tax Year - Critical Dates**:
- **December 31, 2026**: ALL deferred gains from QOF investments will be recognized and taxable. Plan for this significant tax liability.
- New investments in 2026 still qualify for the 10-year appreciation exclusion (gains excluded if held until 2036+).
- No basis step-up benefits remain available for new investments.
- QOZ investing in 2026 primarily provides appreciation exclusion benefit only.

## Requirements

### Qualified Opportunity Fund (QOF)
- Must be organized as partnership or corporation
- Self-certify as QOF on Form 8996
- Hold at least 90% of assets in Qualified Opportunity Zone Property
- File annual certification

### Qualifying Investments
**Three Categories of Qualified OZ Property:**
1. **QOZ Stock**: Stock in domestic corporation that is QOZ business
2. **QOZ Partnership Interest**: Interest in domestic partnership that is QOZ business
3. **QOZ Business Property**: Tangible property used in QOZ

### QOZ Business Requirements
- 70% of tangible property in Qualified Opportunity Zone
- 50% of gross income from active conduct of trade/business in zone
- Substantial portion of intangible property used in active conduct
- Less than 5% of assets in certain financial instruments
- Cannot be "sin business" (golf course, country club, massage parlor, liquor store, gambling)

### 180-Day Investment Window
- Must invest capital gain within 180 days of recognition
- For pass-through gains, 180 days starts either:
  - End of partnership/S corp tax year, or
  - Date gain recognized on K-1

### Substantial Improvement Test
For existing buildings, within 30 months of acquisition:
- Additions to basis must exceed original basis of building
- Land basis not included in calculation
- Original use property exempt from this requirement

## Client Fit Criteria

### Ideal Candidates
- Taxpayers with significant recognized capital gains
- Long-term investment horizon (10+ years)
- Interest in real estate development
- Risk tolerance for emerging markets
- Understanding of illiquid investments

### Types of Qualifying Gains
- Stock sales
- Business sales
- Real estate sales (with complexities)
- Cryptocurrency sales
- Collectible sales (at ordinary rates initially)

### Less Suitable
- Need for liquidity within 10 years
- Low risk tolerance
- Small gain amounts (transaction costs high)
- Taxpayers without significant gains
- Those expecting high income in 2026

## Implementation Steps

### Step 1: Gain Identification

1. **Identify Qualifying Gains**
   - Review recent and planned asset sales
   - Calculate eligible capital gain amounts
   - Determine 180-day deadline for each gain
   - Consider gain character (short-term vs. long-term)

2. **Timeline Planning**
   - Map gain recognition dates
   - Calculate investment deadlines
   - Plan for 2026 deferred gain recognition
   - Consider installment sale timing

### Step 2: QOF Selection

**Option A: Third-Party QOF**

1. **Due Diligence**
   - Verify QOF certification
   - Review fund documents and strategy
   - Assess management track record
   - Understand fee structure
   - Evaluate underlying investments

2. **Investment Criteria**
   - Minimum investment amounts
   - Investment timeline
   - Distribution policies
   - Exit strategies

**Option B: Self-Created QOF**

1. **Formation**
   - Form LLC or corporation
   - Self-certify as QOF (Form 8996)
   - Draft operating agreement/bylaws
   - Establish bank accounts

2. **Investment Strategy**
   - Identify qualifying property
   - Develop business plan
   - Ensure 90% asset test compliance
   - Plan for substantial improvement if required

### Step 3: Property Acquisition (Self-Created QOF)

1. **Site Selection**
   - Verify property in designated QOZ
   - Use IRS mapping tool
   - Confirm zone designation current

2. **Original Use vs. Substantial Improvement**
   - New construction: Original use satisfied
   - Existing property: Must substantially improve
   - Calculate improvement requirements
   - 30-month timeline for improvements

3. **Acquisition**
   - Purchase property through QOF
   - Document all costs
   - Begin substantial improvement clock

### Step 4: Ongoing Compliance

1. **90% Asset Test**
   - Calculate semi-annually
   - First test at 6 months or end of first year
   - Working capital safe harbor available

2. **Annual Filing**
   - Form 8996 with QOF return
   - Track qualifying property percentage
   - Document zone locations

3. **Investor Reporting**
   - Report deferred gain on Form 8949
   - Track holding period
   - File Form 8997 annually

### Step 5: Exit Planning

1. **Before 10 Years**
   - Deferred gain recognized (2026 or earlier)
   - QOF appreciation taxable
   - Consider exchange into new QOF

2. **After 10 Years**
   - Make election for basis step-up
   - QOF appreciation excluded from tax
   - Plan sale or continued hold

## Working Capital Safe Harbor

### Requirements
- Written plan for use of working capital
- 31-month spending timeline
- Working capital used for acquisition, construction, or substantial improvement
- Can be extended for certain government delays

### Documentation
- Written schedule of spending
- Description of property to be acquired/improved
- Timeline for expenditures

## Substantial Improvement Example

### Scenario
QOF purchases existing building in QOZ for $1,000,000.
- Building basis: $800,000
- Land basis: $200,000

### Substantial Improvement Requirement
- Must add $800,000+ to building basis
- Within 30 months of acquisition
- Land excluded from calculation

### Qualifying Improvements
- Renovation and rehabilitation
- Building additions
- New construction
- Building systems replacement

## Risks & Considerations

### Investment Risk
- QOZ locations may be economically challenged
- Real estate development inherently risky
- Limited operating history for many QOFs
- Illiquid investments

### Regulatory Risk
- IRS rules still evolving
- Zone designations could change
- State conformity varies
- Program could be modified

### 2026 Recognition Event
- All deferred gains recognized December 31, 2026
- Must plan for tax liability
- No additional basis step-up available
- May create significant tax year

### Compliance Complexity
- 90% asset testing required
- Substantial improvement tracking
- Multiple filing requirements
- Penalty for non-compliance

### Liquidity Concerns
- 10-year hold required for maximum benefit
- Limited secondary market
- Early exit triggers gain recognition
- Cash flow may be limited

## Case Study

### Scenario
Investor sells appreciated stock for $2,000,000 gain in 2024. Invests $2,000,000 in self-created QOF that develops multifamily housing in qualified zone.

### Timeline

**2024**
- Recognize $2,000,000 capital gain
- Invest $2,000,000 in QOF within 180 days
- Defer recognition of gain

**2024-2026**
- QOF develops multifamily property
- Property appreciates to $3,000,000

**December 31, 2026**
- Deferred gain of $2,000,000 recognized
- No basis step-up (expired)
- Tax due on original $2,000,000 gain

**2034 (10-year hold)**
- QOF value: $5,000,000
- Elect basis step-up to FMV
- Appreciation of $3,000,000 ($5M - $2M) excluded
- Tax savings on appreciation: $3,000,000 Ã— 23.8% = **$714,000**

### Net Benefit Analysis

| Item | Amount |
|------|-------:|
| Original gain | $2,000,000 |
| Deferral benefit (NPV) | ~$150,000 |
| Excluded appreciation | $3,000,000 |
| Tax saved on appreciation | $714,000 |
| **Total benefit** | **~$864,000** |
