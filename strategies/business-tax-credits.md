# Business Tax Credits (WOTC, Disabled Access & More)

## Overview

Business tax credits provide dollar-for-dollar reduction of tax liability, making them significantly more valuable than deductions. Many credits are underutilized because businesses don't know they qualify or find the documentation requirements burdensome. This guide covers the major employment and accessibility credits available to businesses of all sizes.

## Effective Dates & Key Law Changes

| Credit | Status | Expiration/Extension |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC) | Active | Through December 31, 2025 |

## Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC)

### Overview

The WOTC provides a tax credit for hiring individuals from targeted groups who face significant barriers to employment. It's one of the most valuable and underutilized employment credits.

### Credit Amounts

| Target Group | Maximum Credit | Calculation |
|--------------|----------------|-------------|
| **Standard Groups** | $2,400 | 40% of first $6,000 wages |
| Long-term TANF recipients | $9,000 | 40% of first-year wages ($10,000 max) + 50% of second-year wages ($10,000 max) |
| Long-term unemployed | $2,400 | 40% of first $6,000 wages |
| SNAP recipients (18-39) | $2,400 | 40% of first $6,000 wages |
| Qualified veterans | $2,400-$9,600 | Varies by unemployment/disability status |
| Ex-felons | $2,400 | 40% of first $6,000 wages |
| SSI recipients | $2,400 | 40% of first $6,000 wages |
| Summer youth employees | $1,200 | 40% of first $3,000 wages |

### Qualified Target Groups

| Group | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **TANF Recipients** | Received assistance for any 9 months in 18-month period |
| **Qualified Veterans** | SNAP recipient, disabled, unemployed 4+ weeks/6+ months |
| **Ex-Felons** | Convicted/released within 1 year of hire |
| **Designated Community Residents** | Ages 18-39, live in Empowerment Zone |
| **Vocational Rehabilitation Referrals** | Referred by state VR agency |
| **SNAP Recipients** | Ages 18-39, received SNAP in 6-month period |
| **SSI Recipients** | Received SSI in any month in 60-day period |
| **Long-Term Unemployed** | 27+ consecutive weeks unemployed |
| **Summer Youth** | Ages 16-17, live in Empowerment Zone |

### Certification Requirements

**Pre-Screening (Critical):**
- IRS Form 8850 must be submitted within 28 days of start date
- Cannot certify after 28-day window
- State Workforce Agency (SWA) must certify
- Keep certification for records

**Process:**
```
Day 1: Employee starts work
Day 1-28: Submit Form 8850 to SWA
[Wait for certification]
File Form 5884 with tax return
```

### Minimum Employment Requirements

| Requirement | Hours | Credit Percentage |
|-------------|-------|-------------------|
| Minimum | 120 hours | 25% of wages |
| Full credit | 400+ hours | 40% of wages |

**No credit if < 120 hours worked**

## Disabled Access Credit (Section 44)

### Overview

Small businesses can claim a credit for expenses incurred to comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA), making facilities and services accessible to disabled individuals.

### Credit Calculation

```
Credit = 50% × (Eligible Expenses - $250)
Maximum Credit = $5,000
Eligible Expense Range = $251 - $10,250
```

### Eligible Expenses

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Physical Modifications** | Ramps, widened doorways, accessible restrooms |
| **Equipment** | TTY devices, accessible furniture, visual alarms |
| **Services** | Sign language interpreters, readers |
| **Materials** | Braille materials, large print documents |
| **Professional Fees** | Accessibility consulting |

### Eligibility Requirements

**Small Business Definition:**
- Gross receipts ≤ $1,000,000 for prior year, OR
- No more than 30 full-time employees

### Example Calculation

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Wheelchair ramp | $4,000 |
| Accessible restroom modifications | $6,000 |
| Total eligible expenses | $10,000 |
| Less: Floor amount | ($250) |
| Creditable amount | $9,750 |
| Credit (50%) | $4,875 |
| **Maximum credit** | **$5,000** |

## Employer-Provided Childcare Credit (Section 45F)

### Overview

Employers can claim a credit for providing childcare facilities and resources to employees. This credit incentivizes family-friendly workplace policies.

### Credit Calculation

| Component | Credit Rate | Maximum |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Qualified childcare facility expenditures | 25% | $150,000 |
| Qualified childcare resource/referral services | 10% | Included in max |
| **Total Maximum Credit** | | **$150,000** |

### Qualified Expenditures

**Childcare Facility Costs:**
- Construction of childcare facility
- Acquisition of facility
- Rehabilitation of facility
- Operating costs of facility

**Resource & Referral Services:**
- Childcare referral programs
- Dependent care assistance programs
- Contractor payments for childcare services

### Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Principal use | Childcare for employees' children |
| Open enrollment | Available to employees |
| State licensing | Must meet state standards |
| Non-discrimination | Cannot favor highly compensated |

### Recapture Rules

**If facility ceases to be qualified childcare facility:**
- Recapture credit over 10 years
- Percentage recaptured decreases each year
- Year 1-3: 100% recapture
- Year 10: 10% recapture

## Small Employer Health Insurance Credit (Section 45R)

### Overview

Small employers who provide health insurance coverage to employees may qualify for a credit to offset premium costs.

### Credit Amount

| Employer Type | Maximum Credit |
|---------------|----------------|
| For-profit small employer | 50% of premiums paid |
| Tax-exempt small employer | 35% of premiums paid |

### Eligibility Requirements

| Requirement | Threshold |
|-------------|-----------|
| Average annual wages | < $56,000 (2024, indexed) |
| Number of FTEs | < 25 |
| Employer premium contribution | ≥ 50% of employee-only coverage |
| Coverage through | SHOP Marketplace |

### Phase-Out

**Credit phases out as:**
- Average wages exceed $28,000 (indexed)
- FTEs exceed 10

**Full credit requires:**
- ≤ 10 FTEs
- Average wages ≤ $28,000

### Calculation Example

| Factor | Value |
|--------|-------|
| FTEs | 8 |
| Average wages | $25,000 |
| Premiums paid | $40,000 |
| Credit rate | 50% |
| **Credit** | **$20,000** |

## Indian Employment Credit (Section 45A)

### Overview

Credit for wages and health insurance costs paid to enrolled members of Indian tribes (and their spouses) who work on or near an Indian reservation.

### Credit Calculation

```
Credit = 20% × (Current Year Qualified Wages - Base Year Wages)
Maximum Qualified Wages = $20,000 per employee
```

### Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Employee | Enrolled tribal member (or spouse) |
| Work location | On or near Indian reservation |
| Base period | Calendar year 1993 |
| Not owner | Cannot be majority owner |

## Employer Differential Wage Credit (Section 45P)

### Overview

Credit for wages paid to employees while they're on active military duty for more than 30 days.

### Credit Calculation

```
Credit = 20% × Differential Wages
Maximum = $20,000 per qualified employee per year
Maximum Credit per Employee = $4,000
```

### Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Employee size | ≤ 50 employees |
| Military service | Active duty > 30 days |
| Differential pay | Wages paid during service |
| Payment | Must continue some compensation |

## Credit Stacking and Limitations

### General Business Credit Limitation

**All business credits subject to:**
```
Maximum Credit = Net Income Tax - Greater of:
  (1) Tentative minimum tax, or
  (2) 25% of regular tax liability over $25,000
```

### Carryback/Carryforward

| Direction | Period |
|-----------|--------|
| Carryback | 1 year |
| Carryforward | 20 years |

### Interaction with Deductions

**General Rule:**
- Wages used for WOTC must be reduced by credit amount
- Prevents double benefit

**Example:**
| Item | Without WOTC | With WOTC |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| Wages paid | $10,000 | $10,000 |
| WOTC credit | $0 | $2,400 |
| Wage deduction | $10,000 | $7,600 |

## Implementation Steps

### WOTC Implementation

1. **Set Up Pre-Screening Process**
   - IRS Form 8850 in onboarding
   - DOL Form 9061 or 9062
   - Train HR staff

2. **Submit Certification Requests**
   - Within 28 days of start date
   - To State Workforce Agency
   - Track submissions

3. **Track Hours and Wages**
   - 120-hour minimum
   - 400-hour threshold
   - First $6,000 wages (most groups)

4. **Claim Credit**
   - Form 5884 with tax return
   - General Business Credit (Form 3800)

### Disabled Access Credit Implementation

1. **Identify Eligible Projects**
   - ADA compliance improvements
   - Accessibility enhancements

2. **Document Expenses**
   - Invoices and receipts
   - Purpose of expense
   - Accessibility benefit

3. **Claim Credit**
   - Form 8826
   - General Business Credit (Form 3800)

## Case Study

### Scenario

ABC Manufacturing:
- 45 employees
- Gross receipts: $8 million
- Looking to maximize tax credits

### WOTC Analysis

**New Hires in 2025:**
| Employee | Target Group | Hours | Wages | Credit |
|----------|--------------|-------|-------|--------|
| John | Ex-felon | 1,500 | $30,000 | $2,400 |
| Maria | SNAP (age 25) | 2,000 | $35,000 | $2,400 |
| Tom | Long-term unemployed | 800 | $18,000 | $2,400 |
| Sarah | Qualified veteran (disabled) | 2,000 | $45,000 | $9,600 |
| **Total** | | | | **$16,800** |

### Disabled Access Credit

**Accessibility Improvements:**
| Improvement | Cost |
|-------------|------|
| Accessible entrance | $5,000 |
| Restroom modifications | $4,000 |
| Signage | $500 |
| **Total** | **$9,500** |

Credit: 50% × ($9,500 - $250) = **$4,625**

*(Company too large for Disabled Access Credit—must use deduction instead)*

### Total Credits

| Credit | Amount |
|--------|--------|
| WOTC | $16,800 |
| *(Disabled Access—not eligible)* | $0 |
| **Total Tax Credits** | **$16,800** |

At 21% corporate rate, equivalent to $80,000 in deductions

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Missing 28-day WOTC deadline | No credit | Submit Form 8850 immediately |
| Not pre-screening all hires | Missed credits | Implement universal screening |
| Inadequate documentation | Audit adjustment | Keep detailed records |
| Not tracking hours | Credit reduced | Monitor 120/400 thresholds |
| Forgetting wage reduction | Double benefit disallowed | Reduce deduction by credit |
| Missing recapture rules | Unexpected tax | Track childcare facility use |
