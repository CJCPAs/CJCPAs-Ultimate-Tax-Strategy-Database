# International Tax Compliance: FBAR, FATCA, and Reporting Requirements

## Overview

U.S. taxpayers with foreign financial accounts, assets, or business interests face extensive reporting requirements. Failure to comply can result in severe penalties—sometimes exceeding the value of the unreported assets. Understanding FBAR, FATCA, and other international reporting obligations is essential for anyone with foreign financial connections.

## Key Reporting Requirements

### Summary of Major Filings

| Form | Requirement | Threshold | Due Date | Penalty |
|------|-------------|-----------|----------|---------|
| FinCEN 114 (FBAR) | Foreign bank accounts | $10,000 aggregate | April 15 (auto ext. to Oct 15) | Up to $100,000+ or 50% of balance |
| Form 8938 (FATCA) | Specified foreign financial assets | $50,000-$600,000* | With tax return | $10,000-$60,000+ |
| Form 3520 | Foreign trusts/gifts | Any amount | With return or March 15 | Up to 35% of amount |
| Form 5471 | Foreign corporation | 10%+ ownership | With return | $10,000 per form |
| Form 8865 | Foreign partnership | Various | With return | $10,000 per form |
| Form 8621 | PFIC | Any ownership | With return | Loss of preferential rates |

*Varies by filing status and residence

## FBAR (FinCEN Form 114)

### Who Must File

**U.S. Person:**
- Citizens
- Residents (green card holders)
- Domestic entities
- Trusts with U.S. owners

**Threshold:**
- Aggregate value of ALL foreign financial accounts
- Exceeds $10,000 at ANY time during calendar year
- Even for one day

### What Counts as Foreign Financial Account

| Account Type | Reportable? |
|--------------|-------------|
| Bank accounts | Yes |
| Securities accounts | Yes |
| Mutual funds | Yes |
| Retirement accounts | Yes (most) |
| Life insurance with cash value | Yes |
| Foreign pension | Usually yes |
| Bitcoin on foreign exchange | Likely yes |

### What Doesn't Count

| Item | Reportable on FBAR? |
|------|---------------------|
| Real estate (direct ownership) | No |
| Foreign stock certificates (held directly) | No |
| Precious metals (direct) | No |
| Art/collectibles | No |

### Signature Authority

**Must file if:**
- Signature authority over foreign account
- Even if not owner
- Includes corporate officers/employees
- Exceptions for certain financial institutions

### FBAR Penalties

| Violation Type | Penalty |
|----------------|---------|
| Non-willful | Up to $14,489 per violation |
| Willful | Greater of $144,886 or 50% of balance |
| Criminal | Up to $500,000 and/or 10 years |

### Filing FBAR

**Electronic filing only:**
- File at FinCEN.gov/bsa
- Due April 15 (automatic extension to October 15)
- No extension request needed
- Calendar year reporting

## FATCA (Form 8938)

### Filing Thresholds

**Living in the U.S.:**
| Filing Status | Year-End | Any Time During Year |
|---------------|----------|---------------------|
| Single | $50,000 | $75,000 |
| MFJ | $100,000 | $150,000 |

**Living Abroad:**
| Filing Status | Year-End | Any Time During Year |
|---------------|----------|---------------------|
| Single | $200,000 | $300,000 |
| MFJ | $400,000 | $600,000 |

### Specified Foreign Financial Assets

**Includes:**
- Foreign bank accounts (yes, duplicates FBAR)
- Foreign securities
- Foreign partnership interests
- Foreign stock
- Foreign mutual funds
- Foreign hedge funds
- Foreign life insurance
- Foreign pension plans

### FATCA Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Failure to file | $10,000 |
| Continued failure (after IRS notice) | Additional $10,000/month (up to $60,000) |
| Underpayment | 40% penalty on underpayment |
| Fraud | 75% fraud penalty |

### FBAR vs. FATCA Comparison

| Feature | FBAR | Form 8938 |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Filing threshold | $10,000 | $50,000-$600,000 |
| Accounts only? | Yes | No (includes other assets) |
| Filed with | FinCEN | IRS (with 1040) |
| Statute of limitations | 6 years | 3-6 years |
| Extension | Automatic Oct 15 | With tax return |
| Criminal penalties | Yes | Yes |

## Form 3520: Foreign Trusts and Gifts

### When Required

**Foreign Trust:**
- U.S. owner of foreign trust
- U.S. beneficiary of foreign trust
- Any transaction with foreign trust

**Foreign Gifts:**
- Gifts from foreign persons > $100,000
- Gifts from foreign corporations/partnerships > $18,126 (2024)

### Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Foreign trust | 35% of gross reportable amount |
| Foreign gift | 5% per month (up to 25%) |
| Failure to file | $10,000 minimum |

## Form 5471: Foreign Corporation

### Who Must File

**U.S. shareholders of Controlled Foreign Corporation (CFC):**

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Category 1 | U.S. shareholder of SFC |
| Category 2 | Officer/director of CFC with 10%+ U.S. shareholders |
| Category 3 | Acquires 10%+ of foreign corp |
| Category 4 | Control of foreign corp |
| Category 5 | 10%+ U.S. shareholder of CFC |

### Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Failure to file | $10,000 per form per year |
| Continued failure | Additional $10,000/month (up to $60,000) |
| Reduce foreign tax credit | Yes |

## Form 8865: Foreign Partnership

### Filing Categories

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Category 1 | Control (>50%) of foreign partnership |
| Category 2 | 10%+ interest in controlled partnership |
| Category 3 | Contributions of $100,000+ |
| Category 4 | Acquisitions, dispositions, changes |

### Penalties

**Same as Form 5471:**
- $10,000 base penalty
- $10,000/month continuation
- Reduced foreign tax credit

## Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC)

### What is a PFIC?

**Foreign corporation where:**
- 75%+ of income is passive (interest, dividends, royalties), OR
- 50%+ of assets produce passive income

**Common PFICs:**
- Foreign mutual funds
- Foreign ETFs
- Foreign hedge funds
- Foreign holding companies
- Foreign pension funds (sometimes)

### PFIC Consequences

**Default "Section 1291" Treatment:**
- Excess distributions taxed at highest rate
- Plus interest charge from acquisition
- Very punitive

**Elections to Mitigate:**
| Election | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| QEF (Qualified Electing Fund) | Current inclusion of income |
| Mark-to-Market | Annual gain/loss recognition |

### Form 8621

**Required:**
- Any PFIC ownership
- Report annually
- Complex calculations

## Compliance Strategies

### Strategy 1: Voluntary Disclosure Programs

**Streamlined Filing Compliance:**
- For non-willful violations
- 3 years of returns
- 6 years of FBARs
- 5% penalty (foreign residents: 0%)

**Delinquent FBAR Submission:**
- If no underreported income
- File late FBARs with explanation
- No penalty if reasonable cause

### Strategy 2: Proper Structure Planning

**Avoid PFICs:**
- Use U.S. mutual funds
- Direct foreign stock ownership
- ETFs domiciled in U.S.

**Foreign Corporation Planning:**
- Consider check-the-box election
- Evaluate CFC vs. PFIC treatment
- Branch vs. subsidiary analysis

### Strategy 3: Documentation System

**Maintain Records:**
- Account statements (translated if needed)
- Ownership documents
- Trust agreements
- Gift documentation
- Currency conversion calculations

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Not filing FBAR for signature authority | Penalties |
| Ignoring foreign pension | Multiple violations |
| Assuming foreign gift is non-taxable | 5471 penalties |
| Holding foreign mutual funds | PFIC complications |
| Missing aggregation rules | FBAR threshold issues |

## Case Study

### Scenario
Maria, a U.S. citizen, has:
- Swiss bank account: $150,000
- UK brokerage account: $80,000
- German mutual fund: €50,000 (~$55,000)
- Mexican rental property: $200,000
- Gift from foreign aunt: $120,000

### Required Filings

| Asset | FBAR | Form 8938 | Other |
|-------|------|-----------|-------|
| Swiss bank | ✓ | ✓ | |
| UK brokerage | ✓ | ✓ | |
| German mutual fund | ✓ | ✓ | Form 8621 (PFIC) |
| Mexican property | No | No | Schedule E |
| Foreign gift | No | No | Form 3520 |

### Compliance Summary

**FBAR:**
- Total foreign accounts: $285,000
- Threshold: $10,000
- **Must file FinCEN 114**

**Form 8938:**
- Total specified assets: $285,000
- Threshold (single, US): $75,000 (any time)
- **Must file Form 8938**

**Form 3520:**
- Foreign gift: $120,000
- Threshold: $100,000
- **Must file Form 3520**

**Form 8621:**
- German mutual fund is likely PFIC
- **Must file Form 8621**
