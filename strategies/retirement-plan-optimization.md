# Retirement Plan Optimization

## Overview

Retirement plan optimization involves selecting and maximizing contributions to tax-advantaged retirement vehicles to reduce current taxable income while building long-term wealth. Strategic use of multiple plan types can dramatically increase annual deductible contributions.

## Key Benefits

- Immediate tax deductions reducing current year liability
- Tax-deferred compound growth
- Asset protection in many states
- Potential for $200,000+ annual contributions with proper structuring
- Creditor protection under ERISA

## Plan Types Comparison

| Plan Type | 2024 Max Contribution | Employer Match | Best For |
|-----------|----------------------|----------------|----------|
| Traditional 401(k) | $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+) | Up to 25% of comp | W-2 employees |
| Solo 401(k) | $69,000 ($76,500 if 50+) | Self-funded | Self-employed |
| SEP IRA | $69,000 | Employer only | Small business owners |
| SIMPLE IRA | $16,000 ($19,500 if 50+) | Required | Small employers |
| Traditional IRA | $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+) | N/A | Supplement to employer plans |
| Defined Benefit | Up to $275,000/year | Employer funded | High earners 50+ |
| Cash Balance | Varies | Employer funded | Professionals with employees |

## Requirements

### Solo 401(k)
- Self-employed with no full-time employees (except spouse)
- Must establish plan by December 31
- Employee contributions due by tax filing deadline
- Employer contributions due by tax filing deadline + extensions

### SEP IRA
- Any business structure eligible
- Must cover eligible employees (21+, worked 3 of 5 years, $750+ earnings)
- Contributions must be same percentage for all eligible employees
- Establish and fund by tax filing deadline + extensions

### SIMPLE IRA
- 100 or fewer employees earning $5,000+
- No other retirement plans maintained
- Must establish by October 1
- Employer must match 3% or contribute 2% non-elective

### Defined Benefit Plan
- Actuarial determination of contributions
- Must fund annually (within 8.5 months of year-end)
- Minimum 3-year commitment recommended
- Subject to PBGC premiums if applicable

## Client Fit Criteria

### Ideal for Solo 401(k)
- Self-employed individuals with no employees
- Net self-employment income $50,000+
- Desire for maximum contributions at lowest cost
- Want loan provision availability
- Roth option valuable

### Ideal for SEP IRA
- Small business owners with few employees
- Variable income (no mandatory contributions)
- Desire simplicity over features
- Don't need Roth option
- Employee contributions not needed

### Ideal for Defined Benefit Plans
- Age 50+ with high income ($300,000+)
- Stable income history
- 5-10 years until planned retirement
- Minimal or no younger employees
- Willing to commit to annual funding

### Ideal for Cash Balance Plans
- Professional practices (medical, legal, dental)
- Multiple high-income partners
- Need to cover employees cost-effectively
- Desire predictable contributions

## Implementation Steps

### Solo 401(k) Setup

1. **Plan Selection**
   - Choose provider (Fidelity, Schwab, Vanguard for low-cost options)
   - Determine if Roth option needed
   - Decide on loan provision inclusion

2. **Plan Establishment**
   - Complete plan documents before December 31
   - Obtain EIN for the plan if needed
   - Open plan account

3. **Contribution Calculation**
   - Employee deferral: Up to $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+)
   - Employer contribution: Up to 25% of net SE income (after SE tax deduction)
   - Total: Up to $69,000 ($76,500 if 50+)

4. **Funding**
   - Employee deferrals: December 31 deadline
   - Employer contributions: Tax filing deadline + extensions

### SEP IRA Setup

1. **Establishment**
   - Complete Form 5305-SEP or prototype document
   - Set up individual SEP IRA accounts for each participant

2. **Contribution Determination**
   - Calculate 25% of compensation for each participant
   - All participants must receive same percentage
   - Maximum $69,000 per participant

3. **Funding**
   - Contribute by tax filing deadline + extensions
   - No required annual contribution

### Defined Benefit Plan Setup

1. **Feasibility Analysis**
   - Engage actuary for benefit projections
   - Model contribution requirements over time
   - Evaluate employee coverage costs
   - Project funding obligations

2. **Plan Design**
   - Choose between traditional DB and cash balance
   - Design benefit formula
   - Establish vesting schedule
   - Integrate with existing plans

3. **Implementation**
   - Draft and adopt plan document
   - File Form 5500 annually
   - Make required minimum contributions
   - Provide required participant notices

## Risks & Considerations

### Market Risk
- Investment losses still affect account values
- Poor returns can increase DB plan funding requirements
- Sequence of returns risk near retirement

### Compliance Risk
- Annual Form 5500 filing requirements
- Participant notice requirements
- ERISA fiduciary duties
- Prohibited transaction rules

### Business Risk
- DB plans require ongoing funding even in down years
- Multiple location rules may expand coverage
- Controlled group rules may combine plans
- Plan termination can be complex and costly

### Liquidity Concerns
- 10% early withdrawal penalty before age 59Â½
- Required Minimum Distributions at age 73
- Limited access to funds
- Plan loans may not be available in all plans

## Contribution Optimization Strategies

### Mega Backdoor Roth
1. Make after-tax contributions to 401(k) beyond normal limits
2. Immediately convert to Roth 401(k) or Roth IRA
3. Allows additional $46,000+ in Roth contributions
4. Requires plan to allow after-tax contributions and in-service withdrawals

### DB/DC Combination
1. Establish defined benefit plan for base contributions
2. Add 401(k) for employee deferrals
3. Combine for $300,000+ annual deductions
4. Ideal for high earners age 50+

### Multiple Business Strategy
1. Separate businesses with different retirement plans
2. Controlled group rules must be evaluated
3. Potential for stacking contribution limits
4. Careful compliance required

## Case Study

### Scenario
Dr. Smith, age 55, owns a dental practice. Net income: $400,000. One hygienist employee earning $60,000.

### Traditional Approach (SEP IRA Only)
- Dr. Smith contribution: $69,000 (25% cap)
- Employee contribution required: $15,000 (same 25%)
- **Net tax savings at 37%: ~$25,530**

### Optimized Approach (Cash Balance + 401(k))
- Cash Balance contribution for Dr. Smith: $175,000
- 401(k) deferral: $30,500
- Employee cash balance: $12,000 (lower % tier)
- **Total deduction: $205,500**
- **Net tax savings at 37%: ~$76,035**

### Annual Additional Savings: ~$50,000

- **IRC Section 412**: Defined benefit funding
- **IRC Section 415**: Contribution and benefit limits
- **IRC Section 72(t)**: Early withdrawal penalties
- **IRC Section 4975**: Prohibited transactions
